### From mental Ideas directly to Paintings or Compositions

Considering the kind of AI tool I'd like for art creation, my personal wish is for future generative AI to read my mind and transform an “idea” directly into a painting. Because when I paint, my final piece always diverges greatly from my initial mental picture. This discrepancy stems from skills, premeditated planning of the painting layout and style, and the limitations of available paint colors, among many other factors. Mastery in art usually demands decades of practice - time that I admittedly lack.

I often “dream” of paintings, but carving out the time to bring them to life is a luxury I can seldom afford. Sadly, ideas from dreams tend to slip away quickly. Further, when it comes to certain types of fine art, like impressionism, it's nearly impossible to articulate them. A vivid mental picture is often ineffable, as nuances and intrinsic beauty have to be lost in the translation to language. So an AI that can instantly actualize the painting from a person’s mind would be exhilarating. It could also herald an era of increased creativity, as more such elusive art pieces would come into existence.

Indeed, it doesn't have to be confined to paintings; it can encompass other forms such as compositions. I once dreamt of an enchantingly beautiful serenade. Regrettably, its tune swiftly faded upon waking. Yet a thirst to retrieve it remained, and I went on a long quest through countless classical compositions, in search of the elusive tune that had graced my dream. However, none proved successful.
While the precise notes elude me, the dream scene in which I experienced this melody remains vivid: guided by the soft strains of a distant humming, I found myself stepping into a garden resplendent with blossoms, I saw angels gathered, elegantly floating over a majestic lake. This breathtaking scene in my dream remains as captivating as the music it once hosted.

To conclude, I've indeed heard of ongoing research aimed at transposing a person's dream into video form. Though this is intriguing, it also sounds creepy! A more plausible approach might be for me to provide an AI with a rough sketch of, say, a cat, which captures its shape, color, amorphous form, style, and the general appearance that I envision for the cat. If anything is missing, I could further supplement this with detailed text descriptions. The AI could then use these sources combined to deduce a my "dream" piece.
