### Understanding between Human-Human, Human-AI, and AI-AI

#### Human-Human
Based on <i>Sec 3.1</i> in <i>[1]</i> that language can only represent a “subset” of one's thoughts, it becomes apparent why two individuals can't achieve <i>full</i> understanding through mere conversation. Let's label this chasm of misunderstanding as `G-HH` (<i>aka</i>, inter-human gap).

#### Human-AI
Now let’s include AI, which doesn't rely directly on human languages. AI, with its distinctive modus operandi, appears like an alien to human cognition. For instance, AI's approach to scientific discovery from vast swaths of data is a far cry from the method of a human scientist, and currently lies beyond our comprehension, see <i>[2]</i> for example. However, let's not equate our <i>lack of understanding</i> with the <i>non-existence</i> of AI "thought". Presently, we lack <i>concrete evidence</i> to deny that AI has its own "thinking" process.

In LMs specifically, AI endeavours to "project" its thoughts onto the canvas of human language. This process not only gives AI a new cognitive lens—similar to humans, thanks to the mutual influence of language—but it also isn't entirely surprising when AI begins to pick up and showcase abilities that echo human reasoning, as language is a major form of how we humans learn.

###### AI attempts to understand humans
When AI makes an attempt to comprehend humans, it shares our linguistic realm, enabling it to grasp our thought process, a phenomenon termed as "Theory of Mind" (ToM). However, because AI's comprehension mechanism differs from how humans understand one another, we often doubt whether AI truly "gets" us (in the form of how another human "gets" us). Let's designate this comprehension gap as `G-AH`.

###### Humans attempt to understand AI
On the flip side, when humans strive to comprehend AI, we encounter similar barriers due to AI's unique cognition. Still, LMs—AI that has mastered human language—offers us a golden opportunity to better grasp AI's thought process, even if fully bridging this difference remains elusive. By acknowledging that this is a much wider gap than `G-HH`, we admit to an inevitable limit to our mutual understanding that can't be possibly diminished.

#### AI-AI
Contrasting with the `G-AH` quandary, one AI should presumably comprehend another AI more effectively, employing their "native language" that lies beyond human understanding. At one point, I toyed with the idea of AIs communicating using human language—an idea that, upon reflection, seems wildly inefficient. It's simply not the most "natural" language for AI-to-AI communication. For the sake of unhindered inter-AI understanding, they should ideally converse using whatever digital dialect they find most comfortable, to ensure no meaning is lost in the process of translating to and from human vernacular.

#### A word on Vision
I've spoken at length about comprehension through language, simply because of my recent tinkerings with LLMs. Nevertheless, the old adage, "a picture is worth a thousand words", carries a wealth of truth. As I hinted previously, language, despite its potency, has its bounds. If language is humanity's grand invention, then vision forms the bedrock of our instinctual cognition—an innate way we perceive and understand this world. Undeniably, vision acts as a crucial jigsaw piece in this intricate puzzle, offering potentially illuminating insights into the mystery of human and AI cognition.
