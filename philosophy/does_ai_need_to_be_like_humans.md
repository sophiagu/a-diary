### Does AI need to be like humans?

- To truly aid humanity, AI should excel in areas where we humans are naturally deficient. For instances: there're tasks that can be beneficial, yet dangerous or impossible for us to undertake; and there're several kinds of intelligence that we lack. Hence, it's less pressing for AI to master tasks humans can comfortably manage than to become our complementary partner.

- Today, AI is encroaching on human jobs thanks to people who want to make it like humans, but tomorrow, it can also start replacing your family. Seriously no kidding!

- Also why insist on AI mimicking human form? It can look like an alien, and it's more interesting to look that way.

- This last point is mainly about GPT-4. We know that GPT-4 uses RLHF to talk like real humans, mainly to be ethical, but I think it's going too far and is unnecessarily "human-like". For example, if I ask a question where I genuinely seek the truth, not some "ethically sound" responses. For instance, I might ask, based on general relativity, how can we know if xxx exists? Ever the cautious one, GPT-4 knows it can't conclusively affirm or negate. So it proceeds to perform a verbal dance, saying a whole lot but revealing nothing.
